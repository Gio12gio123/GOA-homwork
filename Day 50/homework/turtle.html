Overview

Sea turtles are remarkable reptiles that have existed for over 100 million years. They are known for their long migrations and unique adaptations to marine life.

Species Spotlight

  • Loggerhead Turtle (Caretta caretta): Recognizable by their large heads and strong jaws, they mainly feed on jellyfish and conchs.
  • Green Turtle (Chelonia mydas): Named for their greenish fat, they are primarily herbivorous, grazing on seagrasses and algae.
  • Leatherback Turtle (Dermochelys coriacea): The largest of all sea turtles, they can dive to incredible depths in search of jellyfish.

Habitat

Sea turtles inhabit oceans worldwide, favoring tropical and subtropical waters. They often return to the beaches where they were born to lay eggs.

Life Cycle

  1. Nesting: Females come ashore to lay eggs in sandy nests.
  2. Hatching: Hatchlings emerge after about 60 days, instinctively heading toward the ocean.
  3. Juveniles: Spend years in the ocean, maturing and growing.
  4. Maturity: They reach reproductive age between 20-50 years, depending on the species.
